D. Assumptions verification

By using the plot() function, the ‘Residual vs Fitted’ plot demonstrates heteroscedasticity is satisfied and the ‘Normal Q-Q’ plot demonstrates that the residuals are normally distributed.

E. Adjusted-R2

For the selected model, the adjusted R2 is 0.865, which means that 86.5% of the variance is in penguin weight is explained by species, sex, and flipper length.

F. Coefficient Interpretation

  • sexMale (coef = 530.381): Given all other predictor variables are fixed, if the sex is Male, the weight of the penguin will be 530.381 g greater than if the penguin was female.
  • short_speciesChinstrap (coef = -87.634): Given all other predictor variables are fixed, if the species is Chinstrap, the weight of the penguin will be 87.634 g less than if the penguin was the reference species - Adelie.
  • short_speciesGentoo (coef = 836.26): Given all other predictor variables are fixed, if the species is Gentoo, the weight of the penguin will be 836.26 g greater than if the penguin was the reference species - Adelie.
  • flipper_length_mm (coef = 20.025): Given all other predictor variables are fixed, the weight of the penguin will increase by 20.025 g for every 1 mm increase in flipper length.

B. Statistical analysis of proportions

There is not a significant difference between the proportion of low birth weight for children of mothers who are smokers or non-smokers (p value = 0.135).

C. Discussion of survey methods

The format of the question does not specify the amount that the mother smoked, only that they smoked at all during pregnancy. It would be more appropriate to use an ordinal variable to ask how much the mother smoked during pregnancy, e.g. “No smoking”, “1-5 cigarettes”, “6-10 cigarettes”, “11-20 cigarettes”, or “20+ cigarettes”.

Task 3. Visualizing UCSB campus profiles

A. Goal

The change in diversity of UCSB undergraduates and graduates was compared from 2008 to 2017. Data from 2018 was omitted due to incomplete counts. Undergraduate and graduate proportions over time were plotted separately, and student ethnicity were grouped by white and non-white with ‘Unknown’ ethnicity removed to avoid over representation in either group.

B. Visualization

Figure 2: Total counts of students enrolled in graduate and undergraduate programs distinguished by ethnicity. Ethnicties were grouped by white and non-white. Unknown ethnicities were excluded to avoid over representation in either group.

C. Major takeaways

  • Undergraduate enrollment overall has increased over this period from 17,702 in 2008 to 19,803 in 2017, whereas graduate enrollment has remained relatively constant at 1,982 in 2008 to 1,950
  • Proportion of non-white students has increased in undergraduate programs from 45.6% in 2008 to 63.4% in 2017, whereas the proportion of non-white students has remained relatively unchanged in graduate programs at 33.2% non-white in 2008 to 32.9% in 2017.
  • Proportion of non-white students in graduate programs is generally much lower than the proportion of non-white students in undergraduate programs across all years.
